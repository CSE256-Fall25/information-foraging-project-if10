Parent Handbook


The WUNS Parent Handbook provides families with important information about WUNS’ preschool philosophy, staff, curriculum, classrooms, events, and policies and procedures.



Student Community

Ages: 2 years 7 months – 5 years
Enrollment: ~70% Washington University families, 30% community families
Children represent diverse cultures and economic backgrounds.


Staff

Teachers hold Bachelor’s/Master’s degrees in Early Childhood or Elementary Education.
All certified in CPR & First Aid, active in professional development (workshops, conferences, in-service days)
The school keeps a strong teacher-student ratio ensures individual attention.
Learn more about our teachers


Curriculum

Our curriculum is rooted in play-based, constructivist learning, drawing inspiration from Piaget, Dewey, and Vygotsky.
It emphasizes exploration, problem-solving, creativity, and social-emotional growth.
Children engage in daily experiences across art, science, writing, dramatic play, blocks, manipulatives, and literacy.
We also highlight diversity, celebrating the multicultural backgrounds that enrich our school community.
Learn more about our curriculum

Class Information

All classes emphasize social development and learning through play.

Age requirements:
Two’s/Young Three’s – Must turn 3 by February 1; focus on separation, trust, early social skills.
Three’s/Young Four’s – Builds independence, creativity, phonemic awareness, early math and sequencing.
Four’s/Young Fives – Prepares for Kindergarten readiness: problem-solving, storytelling, classification, collaboration, and multi-step directions.
Bear Tracks – Extended half-day program with art, science, movement, or literacy.
Morning students: Mon–Fri, 11:45 a.m.–2:30 p.m.
Afternoon students: Tue–Fri, 9:45 a.m.–12:30 p.m.
Children must be toilet-trained.


Orientation Day

A short classroom visit with parents is scheduled the day after Labor Day.
Midweek, children attend in small groups before the entire class meets together on Friday.


Parent-Staff Communication

Teachers are available by phone or email after school hours.
Parents are encouraged to communicate openly and in a timely manner to build a strong partnership with the school.


Assessment & Parent-Teacher Conferences

Teachers observe children continuously and keep notes on their development.
Two conferences are held each year: the fall conference is an introductory discussion, and the spring conference includes a detailed written assessment.
Additional conferences may be scheduled at any time by request of parents or teachers.


Classroom Discipline

The school emphasizes positive reinforcement, redirection, and helping children develop self-regulation skills.
Teachers validate children’s feelings, guide conflict resolution, and encourage independence in managing their actions.
If ongoing behavioral concerns arise, teachers and staff collaborate with parents to provide support and solutions.


Policies & Procedures

Parking – 30-minute spots are available by the school; long-term parking is available in Millbrook and Snow Way garages.
Clothing – Children should wear comfortable play clothes and keep one spare seasonal outfit at school.
Snacks – A daily snack of fresh fruit, crackers, and water is provided.
Birthday Celebrations – Birthdays (and half-birthdays for summer birthdays) are celebrated in class.
Holidays – Family traditions are acknowledged, without religious instruction.
Field Trips – Includes frequent on-campus walks and occasional off-campus trips, with signed permission slips required.
Medication & Allergies – Minimal medication is administered at school; severe allergies are documented, with EpiPens used if required.
Nut-Free Policy – The school is entirely peanut and tree nut free.
Accidents – Parents are contacted immediately if serious; all incidents are documented.
Health Policy – Children must remain home if sick and may return only after being symptom-free for 24 hours without medication.
Tuition (2019–2020) – Half-day: $500/month ($4,500/year); Full-day: $1,105/month ($9,945/year).
Drop-Off & Pick-Up – Morning session runs from 8:50 a.m.–11:45 a.m.; Afternoon session runs from 12:30 p.m.–3:15 p.m. Traffic flows east through the parking lot entrance, where teachers assist children at the curb. At dismissal, children wait on benches outside, or inside during inclement weather, until they are picked up.


Special Programs

School Picnic – Families gather at the beginning of the year for “Popsicles at the Park” and at the end of the year for a potluck dinner.
Open House – Held in October, giving families the chance to visit classrooms and meet teachers.
After-School Playdates – The playground is open on select days until 5:00 p.m. for children and families.
Halloween Parade – Children dress up, parade, and celebrate with songs and classroom snacks.
Donuts with Dad / Muffins with Mom – Special classroom play and snack days each semester.
Dine Out Nights – Families gather at local restaurants to share meals and support the school.
Night at the Magic House – A private February evening at the children’s museum for families.
Annual Parent Social – A spring parent-only event such as trivia night, art gallery, or silent auction, supporting enrichment programs.
