Curriculum Overview

Guided by Constructivist practice, we support each child’s social, emotional, intellectual, and physical growth through relevant, hands-on experiences at a pace that fits the child. Teachers pose open-ended questions, encourage independent thinking and collaboration, and draw on our community’s diversity to deepen learning.

Social/Emotional Development

  • Build self-esteem through communication, planning, responsibility, participation, and sharing.
  • Strengthen collaboration, compromise, self-expression, and respect via dialogue and problem solving.

Intellectual Development

Language

  • Speaking and listening; dramatic play; stories and songs.
  • Recognizing meaningful words (names, calendar, jobs).
  • Dictating, drawing, writing stories; making their own books.

Math

  • Shapes, sizes, patterns, sequences; sorting and sets.
  • Counting and 1:1 correspondence; measurement (length/weight).
  • Using numbers as symbols and to represent quantity; graphing.

Science

  • Observing seasons, weather, plants, animals, and community life.
  • Sorting by properties; collecting and observing living things.
  • Conservation and respect for nature; recycling.
  • Growing plants; observing life cycles (e.g., chicks, butterflies, tadpoles).
  • Exploring magnets; changes in matter (heat/cold); cooking healthy snacks.

Social Studies

  • Learning similarities/differences among peers and families.
  • Family visits to share traditions, languages, and stories.
  • Campus and neighborhood field trips; learning about occupations.
  • Experiencing multicultural customs and materials.
  • Understanding disability, empathy, and individual strengths.
  • Role-play related to families, jobs, abilities, and traditions.

Physical Development

Gross Motor

  • Climbers, slides, trikes/scooters/swings; balls and bean bags; bubbles and sand play.
  • Simple games and sport skills (throwing, hitting, kicking, hopscotch).
  • Campus hikes, movement to music, and block building.

Fine Motor

  • Tracing, copying, writing (journals, alphabet, names, messages).
  • Clipboards for writing/drawing in class, outdoors, and on trips.
  • Manipulatives: beads, Legos, nuts/bolts, tops, collage items.

Visual Arts

Emphasis on the creative process: exploration, choice, and expression across media to build focus, fine motor skill, confidence, and independence. Children also enjoy specialist-led visual arts, music, and drama.

  • Drawing (crayons, pencils, markers, chalk); painting (brushes, fingers, found tools).
  • Printmaking (stamps, potatoes, objects); clay and dough for modeling.
  • Cutting, tearing, gluing, stapling; bead stringing.
  • Collage (fabric, paper, photos, small objects); 3D constructions (boxes, tubes, recyclables).
  • Wire, clay, wood, and mixed materials.

Help – Adapting the Curriculum

Teachers observe and assess continually, planning experiences to meet individual and group needs. Supports may include:

  • 1:1 time to understand thinking, build trust, and confidence.
  • More exploration opportunities; simplifying by removing stimuli.
  • Expanding activities with added components or new sensory modes.
  • Individual, small-group, and large-group plans around a concept or skill.

Parent/Teacher Conferences

  • Fall (Informal): Conversation about the child’s activities and progress; Q&A with families.
  • Spring (Formal): Full assessment across the curriculum with observations; shared ahead of the meeting to support discussion.
